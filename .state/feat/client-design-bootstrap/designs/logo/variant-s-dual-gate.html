Design Rationale

The Dual Gate is the more narrative-driven variant. Two hex gate shapes — one representing the human, one representing the agent — face each other with their gaps pointing inward. They are connected by a shared vertical bond at the center: the learning space where both perspectives merge.

The human gate (left) is at full opacity. The agent gate (right) is at 70% — present and active, but distinct. This asymmetry reflects the platform's design philosophy: the human drives the curation, the agent contributes but does not lead.

The lower diagonal connectors (visible at 64px and above) form a subtle downward-pointing chevron where they meet the shared bond. This suggests convergence — two streams of understanding flowing toward a single point. At smaller sizes, these are dropped for clarity, leaving the essential three-element composition: left gate, right gate, central bond.

At favicon size (16px), the logo simplifies to its most essential read: two open brackets with a line between them. This is distinct even at tiny sizes and carries the "two sides, one connection" meaning.
